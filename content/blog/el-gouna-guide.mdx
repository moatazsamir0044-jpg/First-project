# El Gouna: The Complete Short-Stay Guide

El Gouna is unlike anywhere else in Egypt. Nestled on the Red Sea coast 22 kilometres north of Hurghada, this self-contained town was built from scratch on a collection of lagoons and islands. It has its own airport, its own hospital, its own school, and — increasingly — its own thriving short-stay rental market that draws everyone from European kite-surfers to Cairo families seeking a warm-weather escape.

If you're considering a short stay in El Gouna, this guide covers everything you need to know: where to stay, what to do, how to get around, and what to expect when you book an apartment rather than a hotel.

## What Makes El Gouna Different

Most Red Sea resorts are variations on the same theme: a large hotel complex behind a wall, with a private beach, a pool, and a buffet. El Gouna breaks that mould entirely. The town is built around a network of lagoons connected by bridges and boardwalks. You can walk, cycle, or take a golf cart almost everywhere. The Downtown area — a pedestrianised cluster of shops, restaurants, and bars around a marina — feels more like a Mediterranean island village than an Egyptian beach resort.

This walkability is one of the main reasons short-stay apartments in El Gouna have become so popular. When you stay in your own apartment here, you're not trapped behind a hotel fence. You're part of the town.

## The Main Areas for Short Stays

### Downtown El Gouna

The heart of the action. Downtown apartments typically sit above or near the marina, with easy walking access to the best restaurants (try Moods or Sandbar), the Abu Tig Marina, and the beach clubs. This is ideal if you're visiting for nightlife, dining, or as a base to explore. Expect to pay a premium — EGP 1,800 to 3,500 per night for a well-appointed apartment.

### Abu Tig Marina

The marina is El Gouna's showpiece. Yachts bob alongside boutiques and waterfront restaurants. Apartments overlooking the marina command some of the highest prices in town, but the experience — morning coffee on a balcony watching the boats — is genuinely special. Marina-view apartments typically sleep four to six guests and start around EGP 2,200 per night.

### Kafr El Gouna

The older, more traditional part of El Gouna, Kafr has a quieter, more residential feel. It's popular with longer-stay guests and families who want space without the late-night buzz of Downtown. Apartments here tend to be larger and better value — you can find excellent three-bedroom units from EGP 1,200 per night.

### Golf Area

If you're a golfer, the Golf Area is self-explanatory. El Gouna Golf Club is one of Egypt's best courses, and the surrounding apartments are quiet, spacious, and surrounded by greenery that feels improbable given the desert setting. Families with children often prefer this area for its calm and the larger apartment footprints.

## Getting There

**By air:** El Gouna has its own small airport (IATA: HRG, shared with Hurghada) with direct flights from Cairo (Air Cairo, EgyptAir, Nile Air) taking around 75 minutes. Several European carriers fly direct in winter.

**By road from Cairo:** Around 6 hours by car, or 6-7 hours by Go Bus from Cairo's Turgoman terminal. Not the most glamorous journey, but doable.

**By road from Hurghada:** Just 30 minutes by taxi or micro-bus. Many guests fly to Hurghada and transfer.

## Getting Around El Gouna

El Gouna runs on golf carts. Most rental apartments include access to a golf cart, or you can hire one for EGP 300-500 per day. The town also has a network of free water taxis connecting the islands — one of the genuine pleasures of staying here. Bicycles are popular too, and the flat terrain makes them practical.

## Best Time to Visit

**October to April** is peak season for good reason — temperatures hover between 22°C and 30°C, the sea is warm, and the wind is perfect for kite-surfing. December and January see the highest prices and largest crowds, particularly around European school holidays.

**May to September** brings intense heat (38-42°C is not unusual) but also significantly lower prices. If you can tolerate the temperature, you'll find El Gouna remarkably quiet and apartments priced 30-40% below peak rates.

## What to Do

**Water sports:** El Gouna is one of the world's top kite-surfing destinations. The lagoons provide ideal conditions for beginners, while the open Red Sea offers challenges for experts. Diving, snorkelling, and windsurfing are all available. Several reputable dive centres operate out of the marina.

**Beaches:** El Gouna has multiple beaches, some managed by hotels (accessible to non-guests for a day fee) and some public. Mangroovy Beach is the kite-surfing hub. Zeytuna Beach is the liveliest social scene.

**Golf:** El Gouna Golf Club's 18-hole course is impeccably maintained. Booking in advance is essential during peak season.

**Dining:** The restaurant scene is genuinely excellent for a town this size. Dawar El Omda (Egyptian classics), Jobo (Japanese-Peruvian), and The Brewery (craft beer and burgers) are consistent favourites.

**Day trips:** Hurghada is 30 minutes away for shopping. Snorkelling trips to the outer reef depart daily from the marina.

## Booking Tips for El Gouna Apartments

**Book early for peak season.** The best apartments — particularly those with marina views or private pools — sell out months in advance for Christmas and Easter periods.

**Check the golf cart situation.** Many apartments advertise golf cart access but this can mean anything from a dedicated private cart to a shared vehicle. Clarify before booking.

**Understand the utility situation.** El Gouna has its own utility company (ORASCOM). Bills are generally reasonable, but some apartments charge utilities separately from the nightly rate. BirdNest listings include a utilities estimate so there are no surprises.

**Ask about beach access.** Not all apartments have direct beach access. Some include membership to a nearby beach club; others are a golf cart ride from the nearest beach. This matters a lot if beach time is the point of your trip.

El Gouna rewards those who stay long enough to settle in. Three nights is the minimum to appreciate it; a week lets you properly unwind. Whenever you visit, it tends to get under your skin — many guests end up buying property here within a few years of their first stay.
